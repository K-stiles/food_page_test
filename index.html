Ingridients:

  • 1 pound (about 2 cups) dried beans
  • Water
  • Salt (optional)
  • Seasonings (optional)
  • Riped plantain

Instructions:

  1. Sort and rinse the beans: Spread the beans out on a clean surface and remove any debris, such as stones or broken beans. Rinse the beans under running water to remove any dirt.
  2. Soak the beans: Place the beans in a large pot or bowl and cover them with water by about 2 inches. Let them soak for at least 6 hours or overnight.
  3. Drain and rinse the beans: Drain the soaking water and rinse the beans again under running water.
  4. Cook the beans: Place the drained beans in a large pot and cover them with about 2 inches of fresh water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let the beans simmer for about 1 to 2 hours, or until tender. If desired, add salt and seasonings to taste about halfway through the cooking process.
  5. Cook the Riped plantain: Place the drained beans in a large pot and cover them with about 5 inches of fresh water. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let the riped plantain simmer for about 1 hours 30 minutes, or until tender. If desired, add salt to taste about halfway through the cooking process.
  6. Serve the Gob3: Drain any remaining water from the pot, and serve the cooked beans with riped plantain as desired and well ganished. They can be used in combination with a variety of dishes, such as rice, or as a side dish.

Note: Different types of beans may require slightly different cooking times and methods, so be sure to check the specific instructions for the type of beans you are using. Additionally, canned beans can be used as a shortcut, but they should be drained and rinsed before using.
